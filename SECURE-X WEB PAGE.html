// Know The Threats

Threats You Must Know

HIGH 🎣

Phishing Attacks

Attackers impersonate banks, government, or companies via fake emails and websites to steal your credentials and financial details.

  • Always verify the sender's email domain
  • Never click links in unsolicited emails
  • Check for HTTPS and valid certificates
  • Enable 2FA on all accounts
HIGH 🔒

Ransomware

Malicious software that encrypts all your files and demands cryptocurrency payment for the decryption key. Can devastate businesses.

  • Maintain offline backups (3-2-1 rule)
  • Never open unknown email attachments
  • Keep all software fully updated
  • Use reputable endpoint protection
HIGH 📱

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation to trick you into revealing sensitive information or transferring money. Includes vishing (voice phishing) and smishing (SMS phishing).

  • Never share OTPs — banks don't ask
  • Verify callers claiming to be officials
  • Be suspicious of urgency tactics
  • Hang up and call back on official numbers
MEDIUM 🕵️

Spyware & Stalkerware

Hidden software that monitors your device activity, keystrokes, location, and conversations without your knowledge or consent.

  • Review app permissions regularly
  • Unusual battery drain may indicate spyware
  • Use reputable antivirus solutions
  • Factory reset if compromise is suspected
MEDIUM 🌐

Public Wi-Fi Attacks

Man-in-the-middle attacks on unsecured public Wi-Fi networks that intercept your internet traffic, passwords, and financial transactions.

  • Always use a VPN on public Wi-Fi
  • Avoid banking on public networks
  • Verify network name with the venue
  • Prefer mobile data for sensitive tasks
AWARENESS 🔑

Weak Password Risks

Using simple, reused, or common passwords is one of the leading causes of account takeovers and data breaches worldwide.

  • Use a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password)
  • Minimum 12 characters with mixed types
  • Never reuse passwords across sites
  • Enable multi-factor authentication
